Victoria has historically had a low crime rate, particularly in terms of homicide, which has been and continues to be far lower than that of other nations. This visualisation is based on statistics provided by the Crime Statistics Agency in Victoria, which details the amount of crime incidents reported.

As shown on the right, property and deception offences remains the most common offence committed in the past 5 years. It is the only offence category wherein the number of incidents recorded increased from 2021 to 2022. On the other hand, drug offences is the least common offence commited.

The map below compares the crime rate per 100,000 population between each of Victoria's 79 Local Government Areas (LGA). It can be seen that Melbourne has the state's highest crime rate [16427.50] in 2022. Despite this, Melbourne is regarded as one of the safest city in the world, ranking 8th globally according to the Safe Cities Index.

Note: Use the sliders to filter across the years or to zoom in/out the map.

As shown on the left, although the North West Metro and the Southern Metro regions generally have higher numbers of family incidents recorded, they have a much lower rate per 100,000 population compared to the Eastern and Western regions.

The line chart below indicates that the number of offences recorded for most offence divisions have generally increased in the past 10 years.

The graph directly below illustrates victim reports of high harm caused by crime. Examples of high harm includes loss of life, and life-long/severe physical, mental and/or emotional harms experienced after offence. It can be seen that the highest number of victim reports are around the age group between 20 to 40 years for both genders.
